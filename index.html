Portfolio

Client Projects

  • Artists Mentoring Youth

    Artists Mentoring Youth is a Toronto-based performance arts mentorship and training program for gender diverse youth and women (ages 16-29). While troubleshooting and archiving their old website and its content, I led a mini rebrand and developed, restructured and supported the launch and maintainance of their new website. Throughout their site migration and relaunch, I implemented an SEO strategy and ensured brand recognition.

  • Anti-Racism Education Ontario

    Led by the Urban Alliance on Race Relations, Anti-Racism Education Ontario is an ongoing project dedicated to mobilizing community, advancing policy, and promoting systemic change in Ontario education. Bringing a bolder, more modern look to their existing brand identity, I designed an accessible, fully responsive website and resource hub driving visitors to register for the initiative's upcoming summits.

About Me

Gowlene Selvavijayan (they/she) is a Tamil neuroqueer web designer, community organizer, digital communications specialist and storyteller. Born and raised in Scarborough, they moved to Ottawa in 2015 to study journalism and human rights at Carleton University. As a J-School graduate and communications professional of over eight years, they are no stranger to working with trauma-informed, cultural humility and a keen editorial eye while managing multiple overlapping priorities in high-pressure environments with quick turnarounds.

Gowlene is committed to the ongoing learning, practicing and embedding of anti-racism, equity and accessibility in web design and development, communications, community programming and internal policies and practices. In their work within academia, non-profits and the public service and as a freelancer, they have worked to strengthen the impact of research, academic scholarship, and community-based knowledge and make data, information and stories more accessible, compelling, practical, and understandable. Their work and strategic advice have supported organizations in fostering safety, community, knowledge sharing, and greater access to resources, especially for racialized, disabled, neurodivergent, queer, trans and gender diverse youth.

Gowlene takes pride in their curiosity, creativity, and ability to quickly learn new skills and technology to see a project to its utmost potential, foster empowered collaboration, streamline internal processes, and support organizational growth and transformation.
